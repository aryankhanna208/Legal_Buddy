The harrowing acid attack case before the Supreme Court unveils a stark reality of gender-based violence within familial settings. The accused, Sushila's husband, targeted her and their daughters with the sinister motive of property acquisition. This incident sheds light on the vulnerabilities faced by women within domestic spheres and the urgent need for legal frameworks to safeguard them from such heinous crimes. Analyzing this case from a gender justice perspective, it becomes imperative to question the adequacy of existing legal protections and explore avenues for strengthening laws against domestic violence and property-related crimes, ensuring a more robust shield for women facing such perilous circumstances.
