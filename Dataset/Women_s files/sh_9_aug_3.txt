In a distressing incident, a Chennai lift operator faces charges of sexually abusing two juvenile girls in a housing complex. Promptly arrested after allegedly abusing a seven-year-old in the lift, the arrest followed heightened community awareness when the child's reluctance to use the elevator raised concerns. Further investigation revealed inappropriate conduct with an eight-year-old girl, leading to two separate cases under the Protection of Children from Sexual Offences (POCSO) Act. This alarming case emphasizes the vulnerability of minors in seemingly secure spaces, underscoring the importance of community vigilance and the swift legal response to address and prevent such heinous acts against the most vulnerable members of society.
